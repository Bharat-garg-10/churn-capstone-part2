# Manual Review Cases
## 10 Customers With Ambiguous Retention Decisions

---

### Case 1 — Customer: CUST02038
**Segment assigned**: At-Risk High Value
**Signals**:
- Recency: 88 days (long inactivity)
- Monetary: ₹12,400 (top 10% spender)
- Tickets: 0 (no complaints)
- Recent sessions: 0

**Decision conflict**: High value, long inactive, but zero complaints and no app activity. Could be a seasonal buyer rather than churner.

**Recommendation**: Send personalised win-back email referencing their past purchases. Do NOT assume they are churning — check if they ordered seasonally in prior years.

### Case 2 — Customer: CUST01979
**Segment assigned**: High-Value but Unhappy
**Signals**:
- Monetary: ₹9,800
- Ticket count: 5 (high)
- Ticket topics: 3x delivery delay, 2x product quality
- Recency: 14 days (still ordering recently!)

**Decision conflict**: Still ordering despite many complaints — loyal but frustrated.

**Recommendation**: Escalate to senior CX immediately. This customer is at breaking point. Resolve all open tickets before any promotional outreach.

### Case 3 — Customer: CUST00856
**Segment assigned**: New Customers
**Signals**:
- Frequency: 2 orders
- Monetary: ₹5,000 (high for a new customer)
- Recency: 10 days
- Return rate: 50% (returned 1 out of 2 orders)

**Decision conflict**: High initial spend, but already showing discount/return sensitivity.

**Recommendation**: Delay sending promotional discounts. Instead, send a "How to use" product guide for the item they kept to build brand value.

### Case 4 — Customer: CUST01720

**Signals**:
- Frequency: 6 orders
- Return rate: 60%
- Monetary: ₹2,000 (net)
